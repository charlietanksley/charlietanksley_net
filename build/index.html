* On the chance that you've forgotten your Parmenides, I'll remind you of what Zeno says to Socrates at 128c:

"Still, you haven't completely discerned the truth about my book, even though you chase down its arguments and follow their spoor as keenly as a young Spartan hound."

Things don't end so well for Socrates in the Parmenides (or in life), and Zeno is paying him something of a backhanded complement here. But I like it nonetheless. Socrates is relentless in the pursuit of truth, even when he must know his view is about to be decimated (or he is to be put to death, as in the Apology). I'm like that. Or at least I try to be.
